I’ll admit it: I once sold a coin at a 40% loss because I saw a scary headline on a news site. Two hours later, that same coin hit a new "All-Time High."

I felt sick. I had let my **emotions** drive the car, and they drove me right off a cliff.

If you want to survive in the crypto market in 2026, you have to treat it like a cold, hard business. You have to replace your "Gut Feeling" with a "Calculator."

## 1. The "Sunk Cost" Fallacy
Many people keep holding a coin that is dropping because they’ve already invested so much. "I can't sell now, I'm down $500!" 
**The Reality Check:** If the project is dead, that $500 is gone. Holding it just prevents you from moving that money into a better project.

## 2. Setting Your "Profit Targets"
Greed is the #1 killer of portfolios. When you see a 50% gain, you think, "It’s going to double!" 
**The Math Fix:** Before you buy, use a **Profit Calculator** to see where you will be at 20%, 50%, and 100% gains. When the price hits your target, sell a portion. Don't think. Just click.

## 3. Risk Management (The 1% Rule)
Never risk more than 1% of your total portfolio on a single high-risk trade. If you have $10,000, don't put more than $100 into a "meme coin." This way, even if it goes to zero, you aren't out of the game.
